🚀 Launching: Beyond Mean-Variance Portfolio Optimiser with Derivatives & Structured Products

After years in financial services transformation — and building on my 2012 MSc Finance thesis at USI Lugano — I've turned academic research into a fully interactive web application.

What it does:

Classical portfolio optimisers stop at stocks and bonds. This tool goes further — it optimises portfolios that include derivatives and structured products (puts, calls, collars, straddles, capital-guaranteed notes, barrier notes) under a behavioural risk constraint you define: the probability of loss below a threshold H must not exceed α (VaR), or the expected loss in the worst α% of scenarios below H must not exceed L (ES).

The framework is built on the mental accounts theory of Das & Statman (2009) and Das, Markowitz, Scheid & Statman (2010, JFQA), extended in my thesis through additional derivative simulations and parameter analysis implemented in R, and now further developed into a fully interactive Python application.

A key insight the tool illustrates:

Incorporating derivatives into the optimisation process can simultaneously improve expected return and provide downside protection — effectively embedding a hedging benefit within portfolio construction itself, rather than as a separate overlay. The optimal derivative weight is endogenously determined by the downside constraint, unifying return-seeking and risk-limiting in a single step.

What the app offers:

📊 Interactive efficient frontier — MV vs behavioural vs derivative frontier, side by side

🎯 Three portfolio perspectives:
(1) Optimum portfolio without derivative — equivalent to the MV optimum, derived from the mental accounting perspective, confirming MVT/MAT equivalence. This is the reference portfolio — most classical optimisers stop here
(2) Optimum portfolio with derivative — same implied risk-aversion λ as portfolio (1), under the same downside constraint — either VaR defined by (H, α) or Expected Shortfall defined by (H, α, L). Shows how adding derivatives or structured products can unlock higher expected returns beyond what stocks and bonds alone can achieve under the same risk constraint
(3) Portfolio with derivative at same risk as (1) — interpolated from the derivative frontier at the same standard deviation as portfolio (1), for direct comparison of the return gain from derivatives at an equivalent risk level (indicative)

📈 Live market data — any global ticker from Yahoo Finance including equities, ETFs, indices, and crypto (BTC, ETH, and more)

🧩 9+ derivative and structured product types

⚙️ VaR and Expected Shortfall constraints

🔢 Implied risk-aversion λ computed dynamically

🤖 AI-powered — each parameter, constraint, derivative type, and result comes with an on-demand AI explanation, and a full interactive glossary covers the key concepts of behavioural portfolio theory

📄 PDF export of full results

🔬 Standard and High precision modes for publication-quality replication

The MVT/MAT equivalence:

When no derivatives are present, the mean-variance and behavioural frontiers converge exactly — confirming the theoretical equivalence proven in Das, Markowitz, Scheid & Statman (2010). Adding derivatives breaks this equivalence and reveals the additional value the behavioural approach can unlock.
