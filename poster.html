01
The foundations

Built on two seminal papers, both replicated in my 2012 MSc thesis (USI Lugano):

  • Das, Markowitz, Scheid & Statman (2010) — the mental-accounts framework: sub-portfolios as goals, each with a threshold H and shortfall probability α; the mean-variance ↔ mental-accounting equivalence and an implied risk aversion per goal
  • Das & Statman (2009) — the optimisation algorithm for derivatives & non-normal returns in mental accounts, tested on 9 structured instruments
  • My thesis replicated both methodologies and extended the research to further optimisation scenarios — varying instrument parameters to confirm the equivalence and implied risk aversions, and to show the potential benefits of adding complex instruments with non-normal return distributions
Selected figures from my thesis — implied risk-aversion surface, copula dependence, and the bivariate-normal return density.
02
The new automated optimiser

My R thesis tool is now re-built and fully automated in Python: an exact grid optimiser that runs every simulation — on the thesis inputs or real market data — reproduces all the thesis results, and adds AI-powered explanations throughout.

  • Three risk approaches — VaR, thesis-faithful ES, and a rigorous ES modeeach with a configurable loss threshold and probability of loss
  • Four precision modes — Fast / Standard / High + a custom Turbo solver (~60×)from a 21×15 grid (Fast) up to 51×99 (High) — over 20 billion state combinations at full resolution
  • Enhances the optimisation by up to +2.4 pp of return for the same risk level

05
★ NEW — Monte-Carlo + CVaR optimiser, built for institutional-scale portfolios

The grid is exact but its state space grows as mᴺ — impossible past ~4 assets. The new engine instead samples S joint scenarios through a copula and solves the goal as a linear program.

  • Cost is linear in the number of assets — and free in the number of scenarios
  • Several derivatives at once, even on different underlyings
  • Gaussian or Student-t copula for tail dependence (assets crash together)
  • Coherent Expected-Shortfall floor via Rockafellar–Uryasev
max  E[r]  s.t.  ζ + 1/(αS)·Σ zs ≤ −L,  zs ≥ −w·Rs − ζ,  Σw = 1

The result: a goals-based optimiser at institutional scale — dozens of holdings and several overlays priced consistently, solved in a single, warm-started linear program.

A representative six-asset run — AAPL, MSFT, JPM, TLT, XLE, GLD plus a protective AAPL put, 10,000 Gaussian-copula scenarios. Each dot is the maximum expected return achievable for one Expected-Shortfall floor L; ★ marks the chosen optimum (L = −20%, α = 5%).

06
Validated, not just built
  • On the thesis case, Monte-Carlo optima sit on the exact grid mean-variance frontier to ~2–3 bp
  • Scenario Expected-Shortfall matches the closed-form Normal value (e.g. −28.4% vs −28.6%)
  • The frontier is monotone, the ES floor binds exactly, and infeasible floors are flagged
  • Approximate-but-scalable engine that complements the exact reference — never replaces it
